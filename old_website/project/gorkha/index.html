Gorkha Earthquake

Jul 12, 2021

The shaking from the 2015 Gorkha Earthquake caused 25,000 landslides throughout central Nepal. Using ground motion simulations, we study the effects of high mountainous terrain on the seismic wavefield and how this influences landslide distribution. Topography is known to both amplify the seismic wavefield through focusing of seismic waves in ridges and de-amplify the wavefield by scatting seismic waves in valleys. Although topographic amplification has been thoroughly studied in both field studies and numerical modeling, it is not well understood for large earthquakes with complex rupture properties. This study illuminates how topographic amplification is distributed for a large earthquake as well as how this effects where landslides initiate during seismic shaking, at both a large and local scale.
