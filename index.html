Audrey Dunham

PhD Candidate

University of Arizona

Biography

I am a PhD Candidate at the University of Arizona in the Global Seismology and Tectonics (GSAT) lab using a variety of techniques to study global seismic hazard. The primary focus of my research is using ground motion modeling of large earthquakes to better understand the interaction between topography and the seismic wavefield and how this relates to coseismic landslide initiation. I also deploy and analyze data from large-N seismic arrays to image subsurface structure in regions of high seismic hazard.

See what the seismology group at UofA is up to by visiting the GSAT website!

Projects

Gorkha Earthquake

Using ground motion simulations to understand the landslide distribution from the 2015 M7.8 Gorkha Earthquake in Nepal

Imaging the Forearc of the Cascadia Subduction Zone

2D imaging of the Central Oregon Forearc using a dense array of nodal seismometers

Kashmir Earthquake

Ground motion modeling of the M7.6 Kashmir Earthquake in Pakistan to study the effects of topographic amplification on landslide initiation.

Teton Fault

Imaging and characterization of the Teton Fault in NW Wyoming
