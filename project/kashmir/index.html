Kashmir Earthquake

Jul 12, 2021
Photo from AGU Landslide Blog showing a rockslope failure east of Muzaffarabad

The Kashmir earthquake occurred on October 8, 2005, 18km NE of Muzaffarabad, Pakistan, rupturing a 70km long portion of the Muzaffarabad fault to the surface. This event was the most destructive earthquake on the Himalayan margin in recent history, killing 87,000, injuring 69,000, and leaving 2.8 million homeless. ~3000 landslides were caused due to the seismic shaking from this event, and in this study we use numerical modeling with high resolution topography to investigate the link between ground motions and landsliding.
